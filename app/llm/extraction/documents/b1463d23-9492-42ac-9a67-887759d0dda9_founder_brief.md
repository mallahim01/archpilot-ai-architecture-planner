# Executive Summary (Founder-friendly)

We are building a US-only online bookstore that provides a seamless and clean shopping experience for purchasing physical books. This digital storefront will capture the essence of a boutique bookstore, offering a visually appealing and editorial-like website. Our MVP (Minimum Viable Product) focuses on selling physical books from categories such as self-help, fiction, children's books, psychology, and business, with an initial catalog of approximately 5,000 titles. The aim is to provide customers with the ability to easily discover, purchase, and keep track of their orders, while empowering store staff and administrators through intuitive back-office operations.

The primary target audience includes book lovers in the United States who appreciate a curated, aesthetic shopping space. The platform will offer a simple purchasing process, facilitated by Stripe Checkout, and adhere to a straightforward shipping policy: a flat $15 fee per order with US-only delivery and no additional taxes.

## Top 5 Capabilities

- **User-Friendly Storefront:** A clean, editorial-style website for browsing categories, viewing book details, managing the cart, and checking out seamlessly.

- **Efficient Back-Office:** Streamlined tools for staff and administrators to manage orders, process refunds, and oversee catalog and inventory through role-based access control (RBAC).

- **Secure and Convenient Payments:** Integration with Stripe Checkout for secure transactions, supporting various payment methods like cards and Apple Pay/Google Pay.

- **Simple Shipping Model:** Flat $15 shipping per order within the US, ensuring clarity and simplicity for all customers, with no taxes applied.

- **Robust Search and Filters:** Advanced search capabilities using Postgres full-text search, allowing users to filter by author, price, category, and format.

Success looks like a fully operational online bookstore where customers enjoy a smooth purchasing experience and the internal team efficiently manages operations, leading to increased sales and customer satisfaction.


### Problem

Building an online bookstore that primarily sells physical books in the US comes with several challenges:

- **Limited Geographic Scope**: The store is confined to US-only shipping.
- **Shipping Costs**: A flat $15 shipping fee per order may impact customer purchase decisions.
- **No Tax Handling**: The MVP explicitly does not involve tax calculations or collections, requiring clear communication to users.
- **Simple Fulfillment and Catalog Management**: The system must handle order fulfillment and allow basic catalog management without advanced analytics or multi-warehouse support.
- **Role Management**: Proper role-based access (Customer, Staff, and Admin) is essential to manage operations effectively.
- **Customization vs. Speed**: Balancing the boutique bookstore feel with operational efficiency, particularly in choosing between a custom AWS tech stack or a more constrained Shopify setup.

### Solution

To address these challenges, a focused MVP solution is proposed:

- **Custom Stack on AWS**: Utilizing Next.js with managed AWS services such as Amplify, Cognito, and RDS to build a flexible and scalable platform.
- **Seamless Checkout Experience**: Integration with Stripe Checkout ensures secure payments and facilitates the flat shipping rate, while maintaining a boutique experience.
- **Storefront Design**: A clean, editorial, and visual style consistent with the boutique bookstore vibe to engage customers.
- **Back-office Functionality**: Enables order processing, catalog management, and basic customer interactions through role-based access (Staff/Admin).
- **Comprehensive Data Model**: Covers books, categories, inventory, customers, and order lifecycle.
- **Optimized Performance**: Initial page loads and media delivery are enhanced via CloudFront, ensuring quick and seamless user experiences.
- **Communication and Updates**: Automated email notifications for order confirmations, fulfillment updates, and refunds keep customers informed.

### Business Value

Implementing this solution provides measurable outcomes for the business:

- **Market Reach**: By focusing on the US market, the bookstore can streamline operations and logistics, reducing complexity and potential errors.
- **Enhanced Customer Experience**: A tailored and visually appealing storefront increases customer satisfaction and encourages repeat purchases.
  
  - **Metric**: Expectation to sell approximately 5,000 units initially.

- **Operational Efficiency**: Streamlining backend processes allows staff to manage up to 5,000 book titles effectively.

- **Scalability and Customization**: With AWS, the platform can easily adapt to changes, supporting growth and potential feature additions like digital products or expanded shipping options.

- **Security and Compliance**: Ensures customer data protection, with appropriate security measures in place through Amazon Cognito and Stripe, mitigating regulatory and compliance risks.

These strategic implementations will position the bookstore for a successful launch, maintaining an optimal blend of high-quality customer interactions and efficient backend management.


# Users, Roles, and High-level User Flows

This section provides a detailed overview of the user roles involved in the US-only online bookstore MVP, along with the high-level user flows for each role.

## Roles

- **Customer**
- **Staff**
- **Admin**

## Customer User Flows

### Happy Path

1. **Entry**: Access the bookstore via the homepage or a direct book URL.
2. **Browse/Search**: Search the catalog using filters for author, price, category, and format.
3. **Manage Cart**: Add desired books to the cart.
4. **Checkout**: 
   - Proceed with checkout by providing a US shipping address.
   - Pay via Stripe Checkout with cards or Apple Pay/Google Pay.
   - Note: Flat $15 shipping fee applied, no taxes.
5. **Order Confirmation**: Receive an order confirmation via email.
6. **Account Management**: 
   - Sign up/login to view profile and order history.
   - Optional guest checkout allowed.

### Edge Cases

- **Incomplete Checkout**: Customer decides not to proceed; items remain in the cart for future visits.
- **Order Issues**: Contact support for issues like non-receipt of confirmation email or order status inquiries.

## Staff User Flows

### Happy Path

1. **Login**: Authenticate through the back-office portal.
2. **Order Management**: 
   - Access the order queue to view and update order statuses.
   - Credentials permit initiation of basic refunds via the Stripe API.
3. **Customer Lookup**: Search for customer and order details to assist in inquiries.

### Edge Cases

- **Refund Errors**: In case of API failure, retry or escalate to Admin.
- **Permission Errors**: Limited to order management; catalog edits are not permitted.

## Admin User Flows

### Happy Path

1. **Login**: Enter the back-office securely.
2. **Catalog Management**:
   - Perform Create, Read, Update, Delete (CRUD) operations on the book catalog.
   - Manage approximately 5,000 books across categories.
3. **Inventory Updates**: Keep inventory counts accurate.
4. **Order/Customer Oversight**: 
   - Monitor overall store operations.
   - Access to all customer and order records for auditing.
5. **Store Settings Management**: Adjust configurations as needed.

### Edge Cases

- **Bulk Data Updates**: Use CSV import for large catalog changes.
- **Operational Errors**: If access issues arise, troubleshoot or seek technical support.

## Open Questions

- **Guest Checkout**: Confirmation needed if guest checkout requires account creation at any stage.
- **Catalog Management Permissions**: Clarification on whether Staff can handle limited catalog management tasks.
- **Exact Flow Preferences**: Define whether the flow should enforce login before adding items to cart. 

## Assumptions

- **Customer Engagement**: Inline sales tracking or reviews are not within MVP scope.
- **Staff Role**: Primarily focused on order and basic refund management only.

## Budget, Timeline, and Tradeoffs

### Timeline Estimate
The estimated development timeline for the MVP of the US-only online bookstore is approximately 4-6 months. This range assumes initial discovery to deployment, focusing on core functionality and defined scope.

### Budget Bands

- **Low Budget**
  - **Estimate**: $50,000 - $80,000
  - **Scope**: Strict MVP features only; basic Next.js with AWS services.
  - **Tech Stack**: Utilizes Next.js, AWS Amplify, RDS, SES, S3, Cognito.
  - **Tradeoffs**: Limited design customization and minimal flexibility for feature expansion; reliance on third-party services without extensive custom integrations.

- **Medium Budget**
  - **Estimate**: $80,000 - $120,000
  - **Scope**: MVP with additional enhancements; moderate UI/UX customizations.
  - **Tech Stack**: Enhanced AWS integrations, Stripe Checkout customization.
  - **Tradeoffs**: Some constraints on custom feature development; balanced between cost and feature richness.

- **High Budget**
  - **Estimate**: $120,000 - $200,000+
  - **Scope**: Comprehensive feature set including advanced UI/UX, scalability considerations.
  - **Tech Stack**: Full use of AWS services with custom configurations, potential for bespoke system components.
  - **Tradeoffs**: Requires more time and resources; potential overhead from complex integrations.

### Tradeoffs

- **Hosted vs. Custom Checkout**: 
  - **Hosted (Stripe Checkout)**: Faster implementation; may limit UI consistency with editorial design.
  - **Custom (In-app Checkout)**: Allows more control over the boutique feel; longer development cycle.

- **AWS vs. Shopify**:
  - **AWS**: Greater flexibility in custom features and stack control; may require higher expertise and maintenance.
  - **Shopify**: Rapid deployment using templates; may restrict unique branding and functionality flexibility.
